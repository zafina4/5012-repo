Software design is critical because it directly impacts how users interact with a product, it ensures the product’s functionality and usability. Strong software design is the base for a product’s long term success and its maintainability. Ultimately, Pinterest exemplifies how thoughtful software design can drive both engagement and user satisfaction.

Writing Assignment 2

The requirements gathering stage is the first phase of the software development process. This is the stage where all the required functionalities and operations of the software are documented, with very close and frequent communication with the client. The clients and/or the stakeholders discuss exactly what they want to see in the software and why they want it. This is all documented in a requirements table which will then serve as a blueprint to begin the developmental process. As this stage is the base for the software, it is extremely important. The main discussion between the clients and the developers is crucial to avoid misunderstandings and unnecessary requirements that may cause requirements bloat or other problems. It is a strong starting point for the project with a clear understanding of what the client is wanting. It is important to make sure all expectations are valid and can be met with a clear map of the process.

Writing Assignment 4

Communication is crucial in software design especially during the initial developmental stages. Clear communication during the beginning stages of a product development process assures an accurate result. Communication ensures that the vision for the particular product aligns with all that are involved in the project like the developers, clients, stakeholders. It is also extremely important in avoiding misunderstandings and enhancing project efficiency. Key elements of effective communication include adaptability, body language and active listening. The requirements and vision for a project may change depending on the functionality of the product and the client's wishes, therefore adaptability is important to keep an open mind and respond to changes effectively. Proper body language and active listening techniques are also an important part of communicating with clients. Non-verbal clues such as facial expressions, gestures, and eye contact can help make clients more at ease and comfortable to discuss certain issues. It can also be a sign of respect and willingness to collaborate.

A personal experience I can share regarding a miscommunication issue is when I was doing an internship in high school at a newspaper. I was given the task to put together a list of the new toll booth fares for all kinds of vehicles for a newly built flyover in a visual layout. The task was given to me by my supervisor at the end of a weekly team meeting over Zoom. I misheard my supervisor regarding how the layout would be made; I thought she said I had to make my own template from scratch, whereas she actually said to use a template from previous years. I did the task wrong and had to redo it later. With the knowledge I have now, I would make a summary of what she asked me to do and confirm with her verbally if it was right. I would ask more questions to be completely clear on the matter.

Writing Assignment 5

A design principle I want to work on for this assignment is simplicity. Simplicity in software design refers to creating design and solutions that are straightforward and devoid of unnecessary complexity. Simplicity emphasises working and perfecting exactly what is needed for current projects and requirements and leaving room for future changes if needed. This principle makes sure that the output is efficient and effective for what it's required to do, ultimately improving both developer and user experiences. Simplicity is crucial in software design because unnecessary complexity causes unexpected behaviours from the software and increases the time frame due to long debugging sessions. When systems are simple, developers can more easily understand how different components interact, enabling quicker identification and resolution of issues.

Google’s homepage is a good example of simplicity in software design. The page has a search bar and a few buttons for bookmarking, reading lists etc. This reduces noise and distractions for users when they come to look something up. It gives them space to focus on the task at hand. This simplicity has been instrumental in establishing Google’s user-friendly reputation. Another software that has many features and many functionalities but still maintains a simple and user friendly interface is the productivity app Notion. The initial blank page users are given to start with have labeled tabs and buttons to help users build their own workspace. It has a minimalist design and modular structure, making it not only look clean but simple to use. In both of these examples, simplicity is prioritized, giving them efficiency by reducing unnecessary complexity, enabling users to achieve their goals quickly and effectively, and ensuring that the software remains intuitive, adaptable, and easy to maintain over time.
